Boardroom Diversity:
Northrop Grumman vs Thales

Jan 2025 · Individual Assignment · SHRM

A comparative study of diversity in the governance structures of two aerospace & defense giants — Northrop Grumman (USA) and Thales (France). Using a hand-built dataset of board and committee members, it combines exploratory analysis with hypothesis testing to ask how gender, age, education, and tenure shape leadership in each firm.

Hypothesis Testing

Do sociological features shape careers?

Gender & Tenure · t-test
No difference
Tenure does not differ significantly by gender at either firm (p = 0.867 Northrop, 0.514 Thales). Fail to reject H₀.
Education & Role · chi-square
Independent
Educational field is not associated with governance role (p = 0.595 Northrop, 0.453 Thales). Fail to reject H₀.
Age & Tenure · correlation
Thales: p = 0.008
A significant age–tenure correlation at Thales (reject H₀); Northrop is only marginal (p = 0.08). Experience is more tightly tied to progression at Thales.
What it means

Two distinct, complementary stories

Northrop Grumman stands out for gender-balanced leadership and more dynamic role transitions, pointing to adaptability and flexible career pathways. Thales, by contrast, has a more highly educated governance body — with nearly double the share of PhDs — and retains experienced professionals in long-term roles, reflecting an emphasis on stability and expertise.

The statistics add nuance: gender does not significantly affect tenure at either company, suggesting equality in role longevity once leadership is reached. But the significant age–tenure link at Thales hints at a workforce where accumulated experience maps more directly onto career progression — a difference that may partly reflect the two firms' national and organisational cultures.
